Where Debt Can Be Allocated

Rehabilitation of Areas Affected by Natural Disaster

Tacloban after Yolanda

The Philippines is no stranger to natural disaster such as earthquakes and typhoons. These inflict suffering on local populations and create life-threatening conditions for the poor. Further attention should be allotted to ensuring Philippine resiliency.

COVID-19 Response

COVID-19 Vaccination

In the wake of the pandemic, programs to improve the country’s healthcare and resiliency to future health crises should be of government priority. This is especially after prior numerous loans, such as the $1.5 Billion dollar loan from the Asian Development Bank (ADB) amounting to the 3.2 Trillion dollar debt the Philippines currently shoulders.

Undermanned / Understaffed Agencies

Certain agencies such as the Department of Information and Communications Technology and the Department of Human Settlements and Urban Development are observed to have more vacant positions than there are staff. Steps to rightsize or correct these vacancies while reducing redundancies should be of priority so the departments may function as intended.

Education

To go places and do things that have never been done before – that’s what living is all about.

Out of all departments, the Department of Education is consistently allotted the most amount of money as per the 1987 Constitution. As such, efforts should be made to address issues such as the teacher shortage, low literacy rates, concerns regarding senior high school among other concerns.
